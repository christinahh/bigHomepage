Personality assessments and models have been made readily accessible to the public in the form of online questionnaires, garnering popularity due to their quick and clear results. My undergraduate thesis is an exploration of the feedback loop generated from the self-report style of these online tests. The website features two interactive components: an investigation of three widely popular online personality questionnaires, and a new assessment that repurposes the self-report format to promote reflection and critical thought. Upon receiving an assessment result, visitors are encouraged to take a corresponding bookmark or sticker as a means of remembering it. An additional informative video summarizes the overall nature of online personality tests, including a commentary on how they rely upon self-input.
